What is Mental Health?

Mental health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act, and helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Taking care of your mental health is just as important as caring for your physical health.


Common Signs of Mental Ill Health?

Feeling sad or down for long periods
Confused thinking or reduced ability to concentrate
Excessive fears, worries, or extreme mood changes
Withdrawal from friends and activities
Significant tiredness, low energy, or problems sleeping
Changes in eating habits or weight
Difficulty coping with daily problems or stress
Unexplained physical ailments
Thoughts of self-harm or suicide

About Stress

Stress is a natural response to challenging or demanding situations. It can motivate us to take action, but too much stress can negatively impact our mental and physical health. Common sources of stress include work, school, relationships, and major life changes.

  • Short-term stress can help you focus and perform under pressure.
  • Long-term or chronic stress can lead to anxiety, depression, and other health problems.
  • Recognizing your stress triggers and learning healthy coping strategies is important.

If stress feels overwhelming, consider talking to someone you trust or seeking professional support.

What to Do If You Think You're Having Issues

If you feel like you may be experiencing mental health challenges, remember that you are not alone and help is available. Here are some steps you can take:

  • Talk to someone you trust, such as a friend, family member, or teacher.
  • Reach out to a healthcare professional for advice and support.
  • Consider contacting a mental health helpline or support service.
  • Practice self-care by getting enough sleep, eating well, and staying active.
  • Try relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or gentle exercise.

Seeking help is a sign of strength. Early support can make a big difference in your well-being.
